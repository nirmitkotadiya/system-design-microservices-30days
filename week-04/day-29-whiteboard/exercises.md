# Day 29: Mock Interview — Design Twitter

**Set a 45-minute timer. Work through this as if it were a real interview.**

---

## The Problem

"Design Twitter."

That's it. That's all you get. The rest is up to you.

---

## Evaluation Rubric

After completing your design, score yourself on each dimension:

### 1. Requirements Clarification (0-10 points)
- 0: Jumped straight to designing
- 5: Asked some clarifying questions
- 10: Thoroughly clarified functional requirements, scale, and non-functional requirements

### 2. Scale Estimation (0-10 points)
- 0: No estimation
- 5: Rough estimates without showing work
- 10: Detailed back-of-envelope calculations with correct methodology

### 3. High-Level Design (0-20 points)
- 0: No diagram
- 10: Basic components identified
- 20: Complete diagram with all major components, data flow shown

### 4. Database Design (0-15 points)
- 0: No database discussion
- 8: Chose a database with some justification
- 15: Schema design, index choices, SQL vs. NoSQL reasoning, sharding strategy

### 5. Key Algorithm (0-15 points)
- 0: No algorithm discussion
- 8: Identified the key algorithm (fan-out)
- 15: Explained fan-out on write vs. read, hybrid approach, celebrity problem

### 6. Bottleneck Analysis (0-15 points)
- 0: No bottleneck discussion
- 8: Identified one bottleneck
- 15: Identified multiple bottlenecks at different scales, proposed solutions

### 7. Tradeoffs (0-15 points)
- 0: No tradeoff discussion
- 8: Mentioned some tradeoffs
- 15: Proactively discussed tradeoffs for major decisions

**Total: 100 points**

**Scoring**:
- 80-100: Ready for senior engineer interviews
- 60-79: Good foundation, needs more practice
- 40-59: Understand concepts but need to practice the framework
- < 40: Review Week 1-3 concepts and practice more

---

## Self-Evaluation Questions

After completing your design, answer these:

1. Did you clarify requirements before designing?
2. Did you estimate scale and let it drive your design?
3. Did you draw a diagram and explain it as you drew?
4. Did you identify the fan-out problem and propose a solution?
5. Did you discuss the celebrity problem?
6. Did you choose databases with justification?
7. Did you identify the bottleneck at current scale?
8. Did you discuss what changes at 10x scale?
9. Did you proactively discuss tradeoffs?
10. Did you finish within 45 minutes?

## Interview Tips

1. **Think out loud**: Interviewers want to see your thought process
2. **Draw as you talk**: Visual aids help both you and the interviewer
3. **Check in regularly**: "Does this make sense? Should I go deeper?"
4. **Be confident about tradeoffs**: "I chose X because Y. The tradeoff is Z."
5. **Don't memorize**: Understand the concepts, don't recite memorized answers
6. **It's a conversation**: The interviewer is your collaborator, not your judge
